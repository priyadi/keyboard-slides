Building Your Own Keyboard

by Priyadi Iman Nurcahyo - priyadi@keyboard.id - keyboard.id

Ingredients: Plate and Case Materials

  • Material for switch plate
    metal plate, 1.5mm thickness, common materials are stainless steel or aluminum alloy
  • Material for case
    common materials are acrylic, polycarbonate, metal

Ingredients: Controller Board

An Atmel AVR controller board with hardware USB is a safe choice, they are:

  • common
  • well-supported
  • powerful enough for a keyboard
  • practically unbrickable

Ingredients: Controller Board, cont.

  • Teensy 2.0. Very popular but not that common in Indonesia.
  • Teensy++. Similar to Teensy 2.0 but with more pins for larger keyboards.
  • Pro Micro. Cheap and very popular in Indonesia.
  • Adafruit Feather Bluefruit LE 32u4. Supports battery and Bluetooth.

Other Ingredients

  • Switches and keycaps
  • 1N4148 diodes
  • Cables
  • Soldering tin

Step 1: Design Your Own Layout

Head over to 'KLE' (keyboard-layout-editor.com), and start designing!

Step 2: Get Your CAD Files

  • Copy 'Raw data' of your layout from KLE
  • Paste into Swill's Plate & Case Builder
  • You will get an SVG, DXF or EPS file.
  • Send the file to your favorite laser-cutting workshop (i.e. LaserIndonesia) to get your switch plate and case.

Tips: use your favorite vector graphics editor (i.e. Inkscape or Adobe Illustrator) to fine tune your results.

Step 3: Assemble the Hardware

  • Install and solder your switches in matrix arrangement
  • Connect each rows and columns to a pin on the controller
  • In most cases, any pins other than Vcc, GND, and RST are OK
  • Don't forget to take note which row/column connected to which pin

Step 4: Generate Your Firmware

  • Copy 'Raw data' of your layout from KLE
  • Go to QMK Firmware Builder and paste your layout raw data.
  • Define your wiring, pin assignment and your desired keymap
  • Get your .hex file
  • Flash your controller using the resulting .hex file

Advanced users: you can download the source files and make further customizations using the QMK firmware.
